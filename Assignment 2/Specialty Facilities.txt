IMPORTANT NOTE: Buildings with a ✨ beside them are mutually exclusive, meaning they cannot be used with other facilities with ✨, including passive bonuses. Some facilities will have other restrictions on their use, in which case that will be mentioned.


Guild Buildings
Guilds are small communities made up of Red Banners with similar skillsets and goals. Guilds require certain admission requirements to be met before joining. Each character can only be a part of one Guild at a time.

Training Facilities
Training Facilities are buildings designed to help improve your character's strengths, knowledge and abilities. Training facilities can be built by characters and then upgraded over time. Training facilities cost gold and a number of weeks to complete, but this time can be sped up by a character with Carpenter’s Tools Proficiency using their downtime to remove a week of construction. Below is a list of current existing facilities:


- Animal Companion Training Grounds (Tier 4)
Used for training animals and other companions.
- Lake Solino School of Trades and Crafts (Tier 4)
Used for learning new Tool proficiencies and teaching others.
- The Lunaria University for Higher Magical Learning (Tier 4)
        Used for learning new spells and enhancing their effects.
- The Crimson Dojo (Tier 4)
        Used for learning new fighting styles and training with new weapons.
- Monastery of Mental Discipline (Tier 4)
        Used for learning psionic abilities, can also be used to unlock different complications for Research. 
- Employment Office (Tier 4)
        Used for progressing at jobs and modifying job related things.

Guilds Overview
Red Banner’s may choose to join one of the local Guilds to help enhance their off duty activities, help their fellow Red Banners, and gain access to unique quests. Red Banners may only join one guild at a time. In order to join a Guild your character must meet the Guild’s prerequisites and must continue to meet those prerequisites to maintain their membership in that Guild. There are two types of prerequisites: Class and Classless. 


To meet a Guild’s Class prerequisite you must have at least 5 levels worth in the Guild’s specific classes, while also not having more classes in the Guild’s restricted classes. 
For Example: The Hall of Warriors requires its members to have a total of 5 class levels in Fighter, Barbarian, Warden, Gunner, or Monk. Levels in Paladin or Ranger count as half a level, and for every level in Bard, Cleric, Druid, Artificer, Wizard, Warlock, or Sorcerer you subtract a level. Rogue levels don’t count against this. 


Someone who is 6 Fighter/1 Rogue/1 Paladin/1 Sorcerer would have a class total of 5.5, allowing them to join.

Classless Guild prerequisites require you to be Tier 3 in one of their associated jobs as well as meeting a Skill or Tool proficiency requirement. These requirements must continue to be met in order to remain in the guild, as proficiencies from Items, Magical Effects, or Temporary proficiencies cannot be used to meet the proficiency requirement. 
For Example: The Criminal Den requires a member to be T3 in one of the following jobs: Theft, Spy, Forger, Poisoner, or Gambling as well as having proficiency in one of the following tools: Thieves Tools, Disguise Kit, Forgery Kit, or Poisoners tools. If your character meets both of these conditions, they can join the guild and remain in it as long as that is true.

	Tier 1. The prestige of the Hall of Warriors carries with it benefits for its members. Whenever you work a job, gain additional gold equal to the Fame Level of the Hall of Warriors (This bonus is not doubled if you are T5 in that job). Whenever any member would increase their Wage Progression, they may instead increase the Fame Experience of the Hall of Warriors. When the Fame Experience is higher than the Hall's Fame Level, the Fame Level is increased by 1 and the Fame Experience resets. A member of the Hall of Warriors cannot gain more gold from the Hall of Warriors Fame Level than twice their job tier.


Whenever a member of the Hall of Warriors gains heat, the Hall's Fame Experience decreases by 1. If the Hall of Warriors Fame Experience decreases below 0, it decreases the Hall’s Fame Level.


Tier 2. Members of the Hall of Warriors can make use of their weapons on their jobs to gain additional GP. Whenever you work one of the following jobs, you can add to the GP gains a roll of your weapon's damage dice + modifiers (including effects like enchantments, psi dice, Great Weapon Master, etc). Whenever you work an approved job, this building's Fame Experience increases by 1 as well. 
Approved Jobs: General Labourer, Town Guard/Bouncer, Performer, General Store, Barkeep, Instructor, Firearm Tech


Tier 3. Fame now comes with additional perks depending on how much the Hall has.
* 5 Fame. The cost to make use of the Dojo or Monastery is reduced by half this building's Fame (rounded down).
* 15 Fame. When working a job approved by T2 you can upgrade one Wage die to a d20.
* 30 Fame. Characters making Strength, Dexterity, or Constitution checks in downtime gain +1.
* 60 Fame. Whenever more Fame Experience would be gained, every member of the Hall gains gold equal to 10x the Fame Experience gained. In addition, the effects of 5 Fame, 15 Fame, and 30 Fame are doubled. 


Tier 4. Members can make use of their unique abilities during downtime. Members may use the following features during downtime to gain benefits, you can only use one feature at a time:
* Rage. A character can gain a bonus to a Strength ability check equal to the number of rages they have available.
* Action Surge. A character may spend an Action Surge to reroll 1 d20 they rolled during downtime.
* Ki. You may spend ki to gain a +1 bonus to one Dexterity or Wisdom check during downtime for each 4 ki spent.
* Inspiring Aura. You may grant one creature (not yourself) advantage on an Ability check you are proficient in during downtime.  
* MacGyver’d. You can grant one creature during downtime a bonus to an Ability check you are proficient in equal to half your proficiency bonus. 


At Tier 4 you can also request a Fame Bounty. Fame Bounties are quests undertaken by members of the Hall of Warriors. When completed, the Hall of Warriors gains Fame Experience equal to the combined levels of each member of the Hall on the quest.


Researching a Fame Bounty requires a DT 100 Navigators Tools (Int) or Cartographers Tools (Wis) check, upon reaching DT 100 a new Fame Bounty can be posted.

Tier 1. The Nexus is the convergence point of ancient powers, natural and divine. These powers change over the seasons and can be harnessed to gain power. The Nexus has a season that changes to mirror the real-world seasons. During a season, offerings to the Nexus can be made to gain Conflux which enhances the Nexus itself. In addition, members of the Nexus gain a benefit for the current Season it is in.


Members can increase the Nexus’ Conflux in two ways. When working one of the Guild’s special jobs a member can forgo Wage Progression to instead increase the current Season’s Conflux by 3 per Wage Progression.


Associated Jobs. Alchemist, Franchiser, Gardening, Stablehand, Cook, Docks, Tour Guide, Barkeep, Artist, and Priest.


In addition, characters can offer plants matching the Season’s Associated Plants to gain Conflux. The rarity of the plant determines the amount of Conflux gained (0.5C, 1UC, 1.5R, 2VR, 3L). 


The Nexus can gain a maximum of 50 Conflux per season. At the end of the Season the Conflux is added to the Nexus, and that Season loses half of its Conflux. 


Tier 2. Members of the Nexus can gain additional harvests from their plants or followers from their Religion based on the current season. If the religion is of the Season’s associated Domain they gain +2 to their follower rolls. If a plant is harvested from the corresponding plot, roll 1d4, on a four, you gain 1 more of that plant. 
Winter: Rocky | Spring: Aquatic | Summer: Land | Fall: Special


Tier 3. You gain new bonuses depending on the amount of Conflux the Nexus has and each Season can gain strong bonuses based on its Conflux amount.
* 100 Conflux. Each Season now has a maximum of 100 Conflux. Working an Associated job rewards extra gold equal to 1/10th the Conflux of the current season.
* 250 Conflux. A member of the Nexus can spend a week of downtime to activate a second Season. This season remains active along side the current one for two weeks, granting all bonuses and can have its Conflux increased as normal. When it ends it does not add Conflux to the Nexus, or have its Conflux halved. 
* 400 Conflux. Each Season now has a maximum of 200 Conflux. The bonuses of each season are doubled.
* 800 Conflux. When a Season ends its Conflux is no longer halved. An additional increase to a Season above the Conflux cap instead grants that much gold to all members of the Nexus. 


Tier 4. Members can make use of their unique abilities during downtime. Members may use the following features during downtime to gain benefits, you can only use one feature at a time:
* Channel Divinity: You may use your Channel Divinity to gain +1 to a Wisdom or Charisma check per Channel Divinity you have.
* Wildshape: When you make a Strength, Dexterity, or Constitution check, you gain a  bonus to the roll equal to your highest Wildshape CR (min 1). 
* Divine Smite: You gain a bonus to Strength checks in downtime equal to your highest level smite available -1.
* Favored Foe: You can add your Favored Foe die to any Dexterity or Wisdom checks made in downtime. 


At Tier 4 you can also request a Divine Messenger. Divine Messengers are NPCs that members of the Nexus form a connection with that has a communication to the forces they serve. Divine Messengers can be called and give members a quest to undertake in service to the divine entity they serve. A Divine Messenger may also allow members to communicate questions and answers much like a Commune Spell. A Divine Messenger can also be used to sway the current dominant religion to one that the members of the Nexus wish, this however can only be done once per Messenger. Typically, a Divine Messenger will also task the members with a Quest. When completed, the Nexus gains Conflux equal to the combined levels of each member of the Nexus on the quest.


Researching a Divine Messenger requires a DT 100 Calligraphers (Cha), or Herbalism Tools (Wis) check, upon reaching DT 100 a new Divine Messenger can enter the Nexus.

Taming a Creature
A creature must already be tamed before being trained in this facility. A tamed creature may not be hostile towards you and is willing to work with you on your quests. Taming a creature requires a number of Animal Handling checks equal to its Proficiency Bonus, its DC being equal to 10+ the CR of the creature (Round up for CR ¼ and ½). The DC is additionally increased/decreased by an amount equal to that creature's cumulative INT, WIS and CHA modifiers added together. For every 5 above the DC, you gain an additional progress in taming the creature. A character can spend downtime to attempt this Animal Handling check. A Tamer has advantage on this check if they are proficient in Nature. Failing this check by 5 or more may sour the mood of the creature towards the tamer, and with 3 failed checks the creature becomes hostile towards the tamer, refusing to trust them again. Tamers must spend gold to feed the creature while it is being tamed, equal to the creature's CR x 1gp (this is doubled if the creature is a carnivore). Depending on the creature’s size, this may increase or decrease;


Tiny - x0.25, Small - x0.5, Medium - x1, Large - x2, Huge - x4, Gargantuan - x8


Creatures from the PHB that are purchased are already tamed by default. 


Taming Pens (2 weeks to build)
Players can craft specialized Pens for their future companions. Pens must be suited to the creature you are attempting to tame, and only the correct Pen for that type of creature will grant its benefits. A creature can be of a smaller size category for that pen, but it cannot fit in the pen if it is too large for it. To gain the benefits of a Pen, the creature must be fed its weekly intake of food for that week. While a creature is undisturbed for a week in a Pen, it removes one failed taming check at the end of the week. While at no failed taming checks and left undisturbed, your next check to tame that creature gains a stacking +2 bonus per every week left undisturbed. If removed from the Pen they lose this bonus. Finally, a Tamer can spend their downtime attempting to repair the mood of the creature by making an Animal handling check (DC 10 + 5 per every failed check), and on a success, removes all failed taming checks. Failing this check immediately incurs another Failed Taming Check.

The Lunaria University for High Magical Learning is a school for those interested in pursuing higher learning in the arcane arts. It allows students to study various schools of magic, learning new spells, and tinker with the fabric of the Weave. 


Passive Bonus. Scrolls are discounted based on faculty. Can’t be used with the Inking Parlor.


Tier 1: You can work at The Lunaria University as an Arcanist if you have the Spellcasting or Pact Magic class feature, making 1d8 + Arcana + your spell-casting ability. At Tier 3 of your profession, you can use the Arcanist d100 table. 


A character with the Spellcasting/Pact Magic feature can spend their downtime and 50gp to train at the university. They may make an Arcana check and gain training points equal to the roll. You can spend these training points on using any of the Faculties, spending points does not require downtime unless stated otherwise.


Tier 2: When you attempt an Arcana check during downtime you may gain a bonus to the roll equal to the tier of this building. When you spend your downtime training at the University you may also increase your Wage Progression by 1 for Arcanist, you can only do this once per week.


Tier 3: The cost of creating scrolls or copying spells into a spell book is reduced by 10%. This can be used even when not spending your downtime at the University.


Tier 4: You gain a bonus to your wages as an Arcanist equal to the highest level spell slot you have.

Faculties can be built on the campus of Lunaria University. Each faculty represents spaces for the study and continued advancement of a specific school of magic: Abjuration, Conjuration, Divination, Enchantment, Evocation, Illusion, Necromancy, or Transmutation. 


Tier 1: At Tier 1 that Faculty holds all the 1st level Wizard spells from that school of magic. These spells can be copied into spell books or made into scrolls with no additional charge besides the regular cost of crafting/copying. 
* Abjuration: absorb elements, alarm, mage armor, protection from good and evil, shield, snare.
* Conjuration: find familiar, fog cloud, grease, ice knife, tenser’s floating disk, unseen servant.
* Divination: comprehend languages, detect magic, identify.
* Enchantment: charm person, silvery barbs, sleep, tasha’s hideous laughter. 
* Evocation: burning hands, chromatic orb, earth tremor, frost fingers, magic missile, orb of recall, Tasha’s caustic brew, thunderwave, witch bolt.
* Illusion: color spray, disguise self, illusory script, silent image.
* Necromancy: cause fear, false life, ray of sickness.
* Transmutation: catapult, expeditious retreat, scribe, featherfall, jump, longstrider.


Tier 2: At Tier 2 the cost for copying spells or creating scrolls of that school of magic is reduced by 10%. This can be used even when not spending your downtime at the University.


Tier 3: At Tier 3 the Faculty unlocks a unique option that you can spend downtime using.
* Abjuration: You can study a field of magic and become an expert at dispelling it. Spend 20 training points studying one of the 8 schools of magic, to become an expert at dispelling that school of magic. Whenever you cast an Abjuration spell and have to make a roll against your studied school of magic you do so with advantage. You can spend another 20 points to change your targeted school but cannot have more than one targeted school at a time.
* Conjuration🌟: You can spend 75 points crafting a Conjurer’s Orb at this faculty. You must spend the required gemstone to craft it. Once completed, any creatures you summon with your conjuration spells gain resistance to the damage type based on your Conjurer's Orb. You can have multiple Conjurer's Orbs but can only apply one per spell.

When you make a Deception, Persuasion, or Intimidation check after doing this downtime you may add 1d6 to the result, after your roll but before you know the result. Once you use this feature, you must use your downtime here again to use it again.  
* Evocation🌟: You can study here to modify your spells to further enhance their destructive power. Choose one spell you know of 1st level or higher. You can spend downtime to enhance that spell, causing it to deal an extra damage die once it is enhanced. It takes a number of points equal to 30 * the spell's level to enhance it. A spell can only be enhanced in this way once.   
* Illusion🌟: You can spend 25 points enhancing one set of non-magical clothing. Once enchanted, these clothes grant you a +1 to one of the following: Disguise kit, Deception, Stealth, Performance, Persuasion, Intimidation, Sleight of Hand, or Forgery Kit. You can enchant multiple pieces of clothing, but must select a different skill each time.
* Necromancy🌟: You can spend 25 points raising dead in this faculty. Using one week of downtime you may raise one Zombie or Skeleton if you can cast the Animate Dead spell. In addition, for each week of downtime you spend at this facility the maximum number of undead you can control by Animate Undead or by Create Undead is raised by one (maximum equal to your proficiency bonus). You must raise the maximum on Animate Undead or Create Undead separately. 
* Transmutation🌟: You can spend 20 + 10 * twice the spells level, altering the nature of your spells. You can change the damage type of one of your spells to a different damage type. Once you change the spell's damage type, it can no longer deal its original damage type. An altered spell can be altered again to change its damage type by spending the points again.

This training ground can be used to hone one’s skill in the blade, create new fighting forms, and learn mastery of weapons.


Tier 1: A creature training a new weapon proficiency in this building does not need to spend any gold to learn the weapon. In addition, a character training at this facility can attempt to learn a unique weapon form, as detailed at the end of this facility. A character can only learn a number of unique Weapon Forms equal to their proficiency bonus. If the character is a fighter, they can learn one additional Weapon Form for every five levels they have in that class.


To learn a weapon form, you must spend 50gp and make a training check, adding the total of the roll to your available training points. Once you have enough training points you can learn one weapon form at the end of that week's downtime. A training check is an Athletics or Acrobatic check rolled when you train at this facility.


Tier 2: Whenever you train at this facility, you gain a bonus equal to the training check equal to the tier of that facility. When you use this facility to train, you can increase your Wage Progression for Laborer, Instructor or Guard by 1.


Tier 3: A character with levels in fighter, barbarian, paladin, monk, ranger, gunner, or warden can add half their levels in those classes (rounded down, minimum one) to any training check they make. In addition, the facility is fully stocked with every type of weapon for training purposes.


Tier 4: Those training at this facility gain access to specialty training areas that unlock more advanced Weapon Forms for specific weapons. In addition, a character can spend a week of downtime training at this facility for free to change one Fighting Style they know into a different applicable Fighting Style.


Characters can spend time at a specific Dojo to develop their own unique Weapon Form. Doing so requires making an Athletics or Acrobatics check using Intelligence or Wisdom. After reaching a threshold of 250 you develop a new Weapon Form (at GM discretion), and the creating player gains the Weapon Form immediately.

Tier 1 (Basic Construction)
	500gp, 5 weeks
	Tier 2
	1000gp, 5 weeks

This monastery allows for creatures to train their minds and unlock or enhance their psionic prowess. 


Tier 1. A character can spend their downtime training at this building. When they do so, they roll 1d20 and add their Intelligence modifier to the roll, training here costs 50gp. The result is how many training points they gain, which can be spent to awaken Psionic abilities, listed after the tiers. A creature with at least one Psionic Energy Die, Telekinetic feat, Gift of the Gem Dragon feat, or Telepathic feat may also add their proficiency bonus to this check. 


A creature can learn a number of Psionic Abilities equal to their proficiency bonus + 1 for every 2 Intelligence they have above 16. To learn a Psionic Ability, the character must meet its prerequisites and spend the needed number of training points. Whenever you gain a power at this facility, you get a +1 to future training/developing rolls at this facility. This bonus can stack.


Tier 2. The monastery provides the materials necessary for training people's minds. When a creature uses their downtime to train/develop at the building, they add the tier of the building to their roll. When you use this facility to train, you can increase your Wage Progression for Docks, Priest, or Journalism by 1.


Tier 3. The monastery allows you to tap into the collective mind of those studying and borrow their knowledge. You may spend 50 gp to gain proficiency in one tool or language for this downtime. Doing so makes use of this building, which means you cannot use other mutually exclusive buildings during this time.✨ 


Tier 4. The monastery opens up trade and travel to the Astral Sea and the planes beyond. When a character works a Job in downtime, they can choose to travel in the Astral Sea while performing your job. When you do so, you add bonus wage dice to your roll equal to this building's tier and roll for a Planar Complication, Planar Complications are generally more dangerous than regular complications but yield greater rewards, Planar Complications require a DC 15 ability check depending on the complication. If a job is only available because of a building (Teacher, Arcanist, Artist, Gambler, Etc) you cannot work that job while travelling. 


Characters can develop new Psionic Powers by spending time developing them at this building. To develop a power, you can spend your downtime to make an Insight or a History (Charisma) check. After reaching a threshold of depending on the strength of the new power you develop a new Psionic Power (at GM discretion), and the creating player gains the Psionic Power immediately. 


A power that would cost 30-60pts to learn costs 80pts to develop. A power that would cost 61-120pts to learn costs 150pts to develop. A power that would cost 121-200+ costs 250pts to develop.


In addition, when this tier is unlocked, a new boat is also added to the Global Docks.

As an action, you can focus your thoughts into a psionic armor that encases your body. Power Forge Upgrade - Smeltery (Built)
A Smeltery can reclaim metals from armour and weapons, alloy metals together, and makes refining raw ore easier. 


Refining Raw Ore - 
When Refining Raw Ore, you gain a bonus to the check equal to the Tier of the Power Forge. FAQ
Q. Can you combine the same item with another version of itself? (Ie. +1 Weapon with +1 Weapon)


   1. In cases where it is a simple +1, yes. This is not an additive bonus but moves the item up one step. So two +1s makes a +2, two +2s makes a +3. Items cannot gain bonuses beyond +3 in this way.


Q. Can you benefit from a combined version of an item and its base version at the same time? 


   1. No. If you combine a ring of protection with a ring of water walking and attune to another ring of protection you do not gain benefits from the ring of protection again. Items retain their Unique effect names when combined. 


Q. What is the cutoff for combining? Is it just where it no longer makes logical sense, such as a flametongue + bag of holding or does a ring have to combine with a ring and a hat with a hat? Can I for example move a cloak enchantment to a hat or a hat to ring?


   1. So long as the item can make mechanical sense. A bag of holding isn’t a weapon so the 2d6 fire damage from the flametongue can’t really work with it. Since wondrous items are kinda uniform in being misc stuff, most are good. Tape the ring to the hat and make it a buckle.


Q. How do attunement requirements work? If I combine an item that is druid, warlock, cleric with an item that is bard, sorcerer, warlock does the item become attunable by druid, warlock, cleric, bard, sorcerer, or does it become just warlock?


   1.  Prereqs carry over and compound, when in doubt the more restrictive requirements for item use carry over. 


Q. What happens to items that have charges? If you combine two items that have charges do their charges overlap in any way? 


   1. If item A has charges and item B doesn’t (so long as they are good to combine) then they will just use item A's charge system. if A + B have their own unique charges, depending on the circumstance they may either combine pools (say if the items recharge the same way and the same amount) or have separate pools for their 2 halves


Q. What happens when a character dies who has combined items?
   1. One week after a player dies, every combined item is uncombined. The player gets back all expended Runick and chooses how the disenchantment progresses, including which items are destroyed and preserved.  


Font of Enchanting Upgrade: Well of Transference - Cost 5000gp/4 weeks


This Well has the ability to move magical properties from items and onto another. A character can use a week of downtime to place two objects into the well, one magical and one non-magical. The character must make an Arcana check (same DC as combining magic items based on rarity), and on a success, the magical item's properties begin to transfer to the new item. It takes a number of weeks based on the rarity of the magic item to successfully transfer. After which time, the non-magical item gains the properties of the magical one, and the magical item becomes mundane. Metal Effects still apply. All other restrictions on Magic Items (Rarity, Classifications) still apply. Items like shields cannot receive effects from weapons, items that detail "dagger" in its effects can only be transferred to another dagger, etc. Items made of a Metal which would lower the cost of Enchanting an item cannot be transferred.
